WatRides

Designed to solve Stratford's transportation issues

The Project

Finding a way to commute to Stratford becomes the responsibility and added stress for upper-year students in the GBDA program. As the UX designer, I created a rideshare app exclusive to GBDA and MDEI students using the HCD design process.​

Duration: 3 months

Methods:Primary Research, Interviews, Surveys, User Persona, Experience Map, User Flow Chart, Wireframes, Mockups, Prototypes, First-click testing, Contextual Interview

Tools:Balsamiq, Adobe XD, Figma, InVision

About Stratford's Transportation

It is difficult to find a place to live in Stratford as the off-campus housing link provided on the university's official site is very limited. Students are taking action by having a rideshare Facebook page along with various cohort group chats to make carpooling more accessible for all students. However, with the gas price increase, it is becoming costly for the passengers to support this system along with other expenses. This has led many GBDA and MDEI students to choose to live on the Waterloo campus, but the inconvenience of transportation increases the financial burden on students.

Process

WatRides followed an HCD process by first conducting user research. After synthesizing surveys and interviews, the problem was identified, and the project moved forward to the ideation phase where potential ideas/ functionalities were brainstormed. Then, the process between high-fidelity prototypes and usability testing was iterated based on students' feedback.

Research

SkateYou

According to a survey conducted in the GBDA student group chat, the majority voted for commuting to the Stratford campus by carpool instead of driving. The results also suggested most GBDA students live at Waterloo instead of Stratford. Many factors such as accessibility to entertainment, leisure, retail, and housing influence students’ decision to opt for the carpool option. In other words, there is more available and affordable off-campus housing in Waterloo whereas there are limited housing options in Stratford. Many students living at Stratford share the same house with their landlords while the off-campus housing in Waterloo provides an entire unit exclusively to students. Additionally, there are many four-month or eight-month sublet groups on Facebook that provide flexibility to students who are looking for short-term housing.

It is important to note that many students prefer to live in Waterloo and commute to Stratford because of the larger community and student population. This allows them to stay connected to their peers and friends, instead of feeling isolated.

Housing options in Stratford are considerably limited compared to Waterloo. From housing agencies such as KW4RENT, ACCOMMD8U, and private landlords, and WUSA’s latest legal service department to protect students, living at Waterloo campus is not only convenient but also safe. Another factor that should be mentioned is first years usually live on campus, but Stratford doesn’t provide on campus-housing.

Moreover, people said that they would rather spend money on transportation for the sake of their social life and living quality in Waterloo. The only participant who was interested in moving to Stratford noted that housing was more affordable over there compared to Waterloo.

"Mainly the expensive housing and lack of part-time job opportunities, I need to make enough money to pay for my rent + food + monthly bills + necessities, that isn't really feasible in Stratford"

- Teegan Tran, 3rd-year GBDA

The Problem Statement

Based on research, the lack of accessibility to rideshare for students from Waterloo to Stratford and the lack of social/community engagement in Stratford are the two major factors that pushed students away from living at Stratford. Currently, there is no existing platform that provides securedrideshares and events information to GBDA and MDEI students. Therefore, the problem statement focuses on:

How might we create a carpool platform for GBDA and MDEI students that provides safe transportation and helps connect the Waterloo and Stratford community?​

Ideation

In this phase, potential features and solutions were drawn on the whiteboard using the sketchstorming method. This step brought ideas to life and helped removed redundant/unfeasible features.

Primary Features Of Watrides

Post rides, book rides, and events were the primary features that were emphasized during the sketch phase. These functions were designed with the problem statement in mind. Post & Book Rides under user authentication guarantees secure transportation whereas the Events section for both Waterloo and Stratford events was designed to help increase the community engagement aspect.

User Feedback #1

Secure UW student Login

Users were fond of the idea of having an exclusive ride share app for Global Business and Digital Arts (GBDA) students. By logging in from the university's system, the app can verify one's identity and prevent strangers from joining.

" Finally we can stop posting on Facebook asking for strangers to take us to the Stratford campus. I always have to cross my fingers that my driver isn't going to be a creepy stranger..."

" Wow, I feel special for being in GBDA rn! I mean having our own rideshare app? SIGN ME UP!!!"

User Feedback #2

Connecting with the Stratford / Waterloo community

Due to the distance between the main campus and Stratford campus, GBDA students from both communities are disconnected from one another. Most importantly, events hosted by the opposite campus are often overlooked because of the lack of a shared platform - Stratford students miss out on Waterloo events and vice versa.

Hence, the Events page features a wide range of Waterloo & Stratford events, aiming to rebuild the gap between the two communities. A list of rides will then be displayed to students who confirm their event attendance.

" lol I always find out about Stratford events until the day after. Nice idea."

" I like how I don't have to worry about finding a ride after signing up for an event."

User Feedback #3

Getting a ride safer, faster, and cheaper

On top of the student verification system, users may find an ideal ride that suits their budget with a simple process.

" The process [of looking for a ride] is straight forward. I like how I can see the price upfront without having to mesg the driver. This saves time."

User Feedback #4

Beyond a driver & passenger relationship / Avoid conflict

Because this rideshare app is exclusive to GBDA students, the app may help the driver and passenger form meaningful connections/friendships as opposed to a one-time interaction. On the other hand, users can avoid awkward encounters with peers that they have conflicts with by checking the driver's profile and passenger's list.

" This is something I need. PERIOD."

" Wow, this will make my life so much easier. I would hate to be in the same car as my ex."

User Feedback #5

Going to events have never been so tempting

Users earn points by attending events. They may use those points to redeem rewards, such as a UW gift card, an on-campus restaurant coupon, and many more.

" I like this feature. It will def encourage more students (me) to attend events."

" That's a really smart idea to get students engaged. I will totally sign up for events to get a gift card."

Issue

Issues #1

Home Page
  • The overall Home Page needs better UI design - it looks too similar to a calendar

Issues #2

Make or Post a Ride
  • There is no option to register vehicles other than signing up as a driver on the login page
  • It's a hassle to switch between the driver and passenger's mode from the hamburger menu in order to make or post a ride

Issues #3

Events
  • The icons need a better UI design; The current design seems outdated
  • The icons should be replaced with more intuitive logos instead of using the UW mascot
  • When clicking for more information about an event, the background of the pop-up window makes the font less visible and readable
  • The process of adding an event is too time-consuming and annoying - only use expandable panels if needed

Issues #4

Hamburger Menu
  • There should be more features other than the driver and passenger toggle button and basic settings, such as including payment methods or Rides history
  • There are no profile settings - How to edit profile?
  • There is no open dispute function, so what to do where there's a problem between the passenger and driver?
  • No contact information to the WatRide's team

Reflection & Prototype

Overall, the rideshare ecosystem consists of housing, transportation, financial cost, and a sense of community. It is important to bring light to this issue in order to build a better future for students at the Stratford School.

Early-stage product = more potential but heavier workload

It was my first time working on a personal project. Although I was able to have more space and flexibility to explore design options and user interactions, I also needed to create mockups for every single screen and justify my rationale to different audiences. It was a lot of work, but I was able to practice working independently and create deadlines for myself to stay on track. Moreover, I learned how to conduct moderated interviews and think-aloud usability testing, which helped me gain valuable insights into the redesign process.
