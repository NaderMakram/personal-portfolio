Soup Surreal Website Redesign

Redesign for Restaurant Solutions

The Project

Soup Surreal is a local business website that I redesigned as a part of my Web Development course at the University of Waterloo. As the lead front-end developer,​ I worked closely with the UX team and created a responsive website.

Duration: 2 months

Methods:Sitemap, Wireframes, Mockups, Prototypes

Languages:HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Tools:Miro, Adobe Illustrator, XD, Figma, Lightroom, Photoshop

About the Business

Soup Surreal ( www.soupsurreal.com ) is an informative platform about the business and its products. The homepage provides lists of general information about the nutritional content in the menu, such as dietary options and local ingredients with no added preservatives or MSGs.

Problem Areas

Main Page

The site is outdated and is aesthetically unappealing compared to its competitor sites. The fonts and colour scheme lack harmony as it consists of white text clashing with the bright orange backdrop in the three columns. The amount of information in the body text is overwhelming as some content is not useful while allowing difficulty for users to navigate the page effortlessly.

Content & Usability

The low-quality images on the background of each page are not visually appealing (e.g. Soup Surreal tab has a distracting background. It is blurry and pixelated). When the background images are low-quality, it could translate to the products and services.

The mobile version of the site is identical to the format of the desktop version. Users will have difficulty viewing the site on mobile as it is too small to read. Many will have to manually zoom in and zoom out, which is not user-friendly..

Sitemap

New Features

The drop-down menu on Menu page

A dropdown menu will appear when users hover over the Menu page. One of the major issues from using the original website was the overwhelming amount of soup options. Thus, the menu page was divided into three different categories, Seasonal, Omnivore, and Vegetarian to reduce the cognitive load.

Text-change and underline effect

Although text changing and underlined text are simple features, they are great visual features to guide users to important information. Similarly, these features are also applied to the Submit button on the Contact page.

Parallax scrolling

The visual appearance of a website becomes more important as users’ expectations towards the web increase. In this case, parallax scrolling offers the ideal setting to tell Soup Surreal’s story engagingly and interactively.

Hamburger menu

The menu bar is replaced with a hamburger menu for the mobile version. This design not only keeps the mobile's user interface organized but also maximizes usability. With direct access, users can quickly switch to different screens and features.

Outcome & Prototype

The aesthetic of the website should be visually appealing and welcoming before visitors read the content on the site. In this case, the decision to use an overall warm colour scheme helped achieve this goal, and the fonts were chosen to match the website’s clean and aesthetic look.

SoupSurreal is a local business that is adored by the local community as well as incoming university students. The business owner’s passion and belief in bringing flavours of the world through clean and organic ingredients inspired the team to design a minimalistic website. Therefore, using a minimalistic theme provides a meaningful and effortless user experience as it eliminates all unnecessary elements and focuses on what needs to be there.
